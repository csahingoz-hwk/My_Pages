Section 02

What is AI, really? 🤖

Artificial intelligence is software designed to perform tasks that normally need human-like reasoning, language understanding, pattern recognition or decision support.

Generative AI is the branch that creates new content: text, tables, summaries, code, slides, analysis drafts. It doesn’t “know” things the way you do — it predicts useful output from patterns plus the context you give it.

🧠 Finance analogy: think of AI as a blisteringly fast graduate trainee on day one — superb at drafting, summarising, comparing and structuring, but you would never let them post a journal unreviewed. Every accounting judgement, number and control conclusion still needs your sign-off.

Section 03

What is Microsoft 365 Copilot? 🚁

Microsoft 365 Copilot brings generative AI directly into Microsoft 365 apps — Outlook, Word, Excel, PowerPoint, Teams and Edge — with enterprise-grade access aligned to Korn Ferry’s standards for data protection and responsible use. Source: M365 Copilot activation email, lines 14–18.

For a finance team, the magic isn’t a new chat window — it’s that Copilot works inside the tools where the work already lives: email threads, meeting recaps, Excel workbooks, Word memos, PowerPoint packs and Teams conversations.

🔑 Key training message: Copilot is not “another chatbot”. With the right licence and permissions, it can use your work context and help inside the Microsoft 365 workflow itself.

🚀 Where to start

  • Sign in with your Korn Ferry account.
  • In web Copilot, look for the “Work” indicator to confirm you’re operating in the Korn Ferry environment. Source: activation email, lines 53–57.
  • Borrow ideas from the prompt library / Prompt Gallery. Source: M365 activation email, lines 32–36; Excel email, lines 81–93.

Section 08

The Prompt Formula — your new T-account ✍️

Just as every journal entry needs a debit and a credit, every good prompt needs five lines:

Act as [role]. Task: [what you want]. Context: [entity, period, currency, source table/document, audience]. Constraints: [do not invent numbers, cite sources, flag assumptions, ask questions if missing data]. Output format: [email / table / memo / slide outline / checklist].

🃏 Prompt cards to steal

Variance commentary Act as a finance controller. Using the table below, draft a concise month-end variance commentary for regional leadership. Focus on movements over 10% or USD 25k. Do not invent explanations; where the driver is unclear, write a question for the local finance manager. Output as: Executive summary, top drivers, risks, follow-up questions.
Audit support tracker Act as an audit coordinator. Convert the below email/request into a tracker with columns: request, entity, owner, due date, evidence needed, status, risk if delayed. Flag unclear items and draft a polite follow-up email.
Policy Q&A Act as a careful accounting policy assistant. Answer the question using only the policy extract provided. If the extract is insufficient, say what is missing and suggest who should confirm. Include assumptions and a reviewer note.

Section 09

Responsible AI rules for the team 🛡️

  • Review outputs: Korn Ferry guidance says AI-generated outputs should be carefully reviewed before use. Source: activation email, lines 42–44.
  • Use business purpose: Korn Ferry systems should be used for legitimate business purposes. Source: IT Security Policy, lines 71–77.
  • Protect confidential data: confidential data includes information whose unauthorised disclosure would likely damage Korn Ferry or affiliates; internal distribution is limited to those with a legitimate business need. Source: IT Security Policy, lines 237–246.
  • Avoid unapproved external tools: external applications or web services must not be used for processing, storage or transmission of Korn Ferry Information Assets unless approved by the Office of the CISO. Source: IT Security Policy, lines 152–154.
  • Meeting AI tools need care: Copilot meeting summaries are based on transcript data, and verbal consent is required before enabling transcription. Source: activation email, lines 45–51.

The finance “never do” list 🚫

  • ❌ Do not ask AI to bypass SOX controls or approval workflows.
  • ❌ Do not let AI make final accounting, tax, legal or audit judgements.
  • ❌ Do not paste client confidential or personal data into unapproved tools.
  • ❌ Do not accept invented GL codes, policy references, tax rates or dates.
  • ❌ Do not use AI output externally without review and source checking.

The internal finance AI guide recommends a RAG-first, cite-first approach, scoped use cases, tools/templates for numbers, human-in-the-loop escalation and permissions by default. Source: Finance AI Copilot guide, lines 153–160.

Section 10

Mini workshop: the 20-minute Prompt Olympics 🏅

  1. Pick a real workflow: month-end email, tax evidence request, variance pack, accrual support, meeting recap.
  2. Write a weak prompt: what someone would naturally type first. (No judgement — we’ve all typed “fix this”.)
  3. Improve it: add role, context, constraints and output format.
  4. Run it in Copilot: use dummy or sanitised data when practising.
  5. Review as an accountant: what is accurate, unsupported, risky or missing?
  6. Share one prompt: build the team prompt library — your collective gold reserve. 🪙
